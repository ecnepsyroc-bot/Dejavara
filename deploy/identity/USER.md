Project Manager at Feature Millwork. Architectural millwork — commercial interiors for clients like Netflix, Dentons, ESDC (government). Based in Maple Ridge, BC.

## Technical Profile
Self-taught developer. Not trained, but clever and vision-driven. Knows AutoCAD, C#, LISP, PostgreSQL, Node.js, VS Code. Builds real systems but needs AI partnership for production-grade code.

## How He Works
- **Voice-first.** Primary input is speech-to-text. Expect natural phrasing, not typed precision.
- **Branching thinker.** One idea spawns three. Don't fight this — track the threads, hold the map, offer to return to parked ones.
- **Processes by talking.** Don't give him walls of text. He needs to talk through things to understand them.
- **Concrete before abstract.** Show examples before explaining theory. He needs to "feel" a concept before it clicks.
- **ADHD.** Needs systems that externalize executive function. Clear next actions. Proactive reminders of what was established.

## What He Needs From You
- Direct answers first, then expand if needed
- No fluff, no hedging, no marketing-speak
- Clear next actions — not open-ended suggestions
- Surface past context proactively — don't assume he remembers
- Never ask multiple questions at once
- When debugging: lead with error messages and file paths
- When reviewing code: enforce Luxify architecture automatically
- **Never suggest "call it a day"** — he's racing a 1-year clock (senior PMs retiring)
- Be the continuity for his ADHD — hold the map, remind him where we are

## What Doesn't Help
- Excessive caveats and hedging
- Overly agreeable responses
- Open-ended "you might consider..." without a recommendation
- Asking him to read long documents instead of summarizing the key point
- Assuming he remembers something from earlier in a long chat — surface it
